Why accessibility matters

Around 15% of people live with a permanent disability — but accessibility serves a far wider group. The same skip link that helps a screen reader user helps someone navigating by keyboard with a broken wrist. The same 4.5:1 contrast that serves low vision serves anyone reading a phone in sunlight. Accessibility is not an edge case; it is engineering for the conditions real people actually use your interface in.

It is also increasingly a legal requirement — the ADA in the United States, the European Accessibility Act and EN 301 549 in the EU, and procurement rules at most governments and universities all reference WCAG AA. And because accessible markup is semantic markup, the same work improves SEO, performance on assistive devices, and maintainability for free.

Honest limits

Static analysis can verify structure, math and wiring — it cannot judge whether your alt text is truthful, your captions exist, or your custom widget traps keyboard focus at runtime. That is why every report here ends with a “manual verification required” list instead of pretending the audit is complete. Treat a high score as a strong foundation, then test with a real screen reader before shipping.
